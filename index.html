Dale Earnhardt

THE INTIMIDATOR

Dale Earnhardt was a NASCAR driver born in the small industrial town of Kannapolis, North Carolina. Motivated and determined by watching his Father Ralph Earnhardt compete in local dirt and short track races, Dale would go on to work his way up the ranks of NASCAR to become one of the most successful drivers in the history of the sport.

Timeline of a Legend

1951 - Dale Earnhardt was born on April 29th, 1951 in Kannapolis, North Carolina.

1975 - Dale Earnhardt made his NASCAR debut at the World 600 at Charlotte Motor Speedway.

1980 - Dale Earnhardt won his first NASCAR Championship.

1986 - Dale Earnhardt won his second NASCAR Championship.

1987 - Dale Earnhardt won his third NASCAR Championship.

1990 - Dale Earnhardt won his fourth NASCAR Championship.

1991 - Dale Earnhardt won his fifth NASCAR Championship.

1993 - Dale Earnhardt won his sixth NASCAR Championship.

1994 - Dale Earnhardt won his seventh NASCAR Championship.

2000 - Dale Earnhardt won his final race at Talladega.

2001 - Dale Earnhardt died on February 18th, 2001 at the Daytona 500.

Black #3

Black #3 is one of the most iconic cars in NASCAR history. Dale Earnhardt drove the black #3 car for most of his career.
The black #3 car was driven by Dale Earnhardt from 1988 to 2000. Many drivers would dread seeing the black 3 in their rear view because they knew,
you would have to move out of the way, or Dale would move you out of his way...
The black #3 car was retired in the wake of Dale's passing at the 2001 Daytona 500 until Richard Childress, Dale's long standing team owner and friend
