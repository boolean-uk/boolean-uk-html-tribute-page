10 Reasons to visit Maldives

Maldives - Tropical Paradise On Earth

Surrounded by deep blue waters, flourishing coral reefs and powder white beaches, the chain of islands that makes up the Maldives provides the ultimate destination to unwind with a delicious cocktail whilst lounging in a hammock or, for those slightly more active, exploring the vivid coral reefs on excellent snorkelling and scuba diving excursions. Additionally, the waters surrounding the islands boast 10 to 12 different species of whales and dolphins, offering excellent viewing opportunities. The islands are also home to some of the world’s foremost resorts, which offer sensational menus, breathtaking accommodation options and soul-soothing spa treatments.

  1. Beaches of Maldives

    2. The beaches of Maldives are lovely to see. On the side of the beaches, people can relax. The beaches of Maldives are very relaxing in nature. This is the main reason or causes to visit the Maldives. There are no traces of littering inside of beaches. People love to have laid-back time on the beaches. The vast bluish-green Indian Ocean kissing the horizon is a fantastic view of Maldives. In the morning, the soft sunlight and calmer waves are very delightful. Visitors enjoy the sea breeze and the mesmerizing sound of the waves that are continually running in 25-30-degree centigrade temperature. It redefines the romance beneath the thousands of stars

  2. Swim with the Sharks

    3. The majestic and substantial whale sharks along with the smaller-sized reef sharks are swimming in water. In the coral atolls and the surrounding lagoons of the resort, these sharks are lurking peacefully in the water. This is also one of the popular reasons to travel to the Maldives. These sharks are born throughout the area. There are some unique prospective places for encountering them up close like Baa and Ari atolls and Maaya Thila. People also wait for watching them when they come punctually during their daily feeding time near some resorts.

  3. Dive into the Coral Reef

    4. People like to diving in the coral reef of Maldives. The underwater marine life includes the manta rays, sea turtles, and barracuda which swims through the vibrant coral reef. This is another best reason for visiting the Maldives. It has prevalent reef systems. It is very often found in marine life like in seas or oceans. Kuda Hurra is famous for its warm water, and people do not need to wear a wetsuit during diving into the coral reef. The reef fishes are very friendly, that’s why people prefer to bring a waterproof camera and use that when they are diving inside the water.

  4. Water Sports

    5. There are many types of water sports in the Maldives for enjoying purposes. Kayaking, Canoeing, Sea bobbing, Jet-skiing, Snorkeling, Rollerblading, also known as X-Jet blades, etc. are some of the famous water sports in the Maldives. People even hire instructors because they don’t learn the skills of water sports. Top resorts always have some instructors for this purpose.The dolphin and turtle safari boats are also popular among visitors still on their list for riding on to them. The person who rides on all types of waves loves the sunshine, monsoon wind, and the atolls’ consistent waves.

  5. Culture of Maldives

    6. The people always cherish the Culture of the Maldives. Bodu Beru is the famous folk dance of Maldivian and often held in the evening on the sides of beaches. This is an eye-catching sightVisitors always love to view this. While at the time of watching, people also try to move their legs and try to dance.This is the fun part for people who visit this country. Maldivian boat Dhoni is very charming for riding on this and get to know about local people and their culture.

  6. The Cuisine

    7. The foods of Maldives are one of the best reasons for visiting this place. The tastes of the food are very tasty and delicious. The local food is Maldivian Curry with Tuna by the placid blue lagoon. Visitors enjoy the food with the intoxicating sea breeze.Garudiya is a fish soup served with lime, chilli, rice, curry leaves, onions, and coconut. The famous food Mashumi Roshi Tuna and Chapati and the Hedhika Tuna, egg, potato, and coconut are worth trying.Street food Mas Kashi which is dried fish along with mango and coconut, are famous street foods.

  7. Spa Therapy

    8. The Maldives is very well-acknowledged for its spa therapies. In underwater Huvafen Fushi people always pay a visit here for getting seaside massages. Visitors still have a massage when they visit this country.Peoples are being pampered and renovate by the reiki of masseuse and enjoying the panoramic view of the marine life in the house reef of the resort.Kuda Huraa is a fantastic place for getting a foot massage or full-body treatment which heals all your body pain and enjoys a massage with a sip of ginger tea.

  8. Bioluminescent Water

    9. Watching bioluminescent water under the stars is a great thing about the Maldives. Vaadhoo island located in Raa Atoll is very famous for this; here, one can see the stars that are getting resentful of the Playful Sea of stars of the Indian Ocean.The microscopic marine algae and dinoflagellates emit the luminescent toxins with waves that are their defense mechanism that keeps away fish and other creatures in the water.People love to walk through them and create more luminescence, a memory designed for a lifetime.

  9. The Architectural Heritages

    10. The Maldives is dominating the skyline of males. On the grand Friday mosque located at the Islamic center with a glistening golden dome, Arabic calligraphy and wood carvings are beautifully architect on the walls. The library is most extensive in the Maldives.There is a beautiful view of the ornate chandeliers, intricately and delicately wooden carpet, and the carved wooden door and side panel, and visitors love to see this.Between the time of 2 PM to 3 PM is the best time for visiting this place as there is less crowd during this time. There are many mosques which are a great example of the best coral stonework.

  10. Male and Suburbs

    11. Male is the capital of Maldives and its most populous city of Maldives. Male and Suburbs play a significant role in the purest essence of the Maldivian lifestyle.People visit the prison and take a royal bath at Villingili or go to the Hulhumale to swim with the fishes through a coral atoll.People also take Scooty on rent and ride through the less-explored nooks and the corners of males.

    Conclusion:
    The Maldives is a very famous destination for vacations. The country is quite expensive, but it is gorgeous. The beaches of this country are renowned across the world. The weather is also delightful.
