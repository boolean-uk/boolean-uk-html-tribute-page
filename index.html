A Tribute to Elon Musk

Early Life

Elon Musk was born on June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa. He developed an interest in computing while attending Waterkloof House Preparatory School. At age 10, he got his first computer and learned to program. Musk was born to a South African father and a Canadian mother. He displayed an early talent for computers and entrepreneurship. At age 12 he created a video game and sold it to a computer magazine. In 1988, after obtaining a Canadian passport, Musk left South Africa because he was unwilling to support apartheid through compulsory military service and because he sought the greater economic opportunities available in the United States.

Entrepreneurial Ventures

Throughout his career, Musk has founded, co-founded, or led several high-profile technology companies. He started Zip2, a business directory and map service, which was sold to Compaq for nearly $300 million in 1999. He then co-founded X.com, an online payment company, which later became PayPal and was subsequently acquired by eBay for $1.5 billion in stock.

Notably, Musk also leads SpaceX, Tesla, Inc., Neuralink, and The Boring Company.
